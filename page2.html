Our stoy

Our BSF farming journey began with an innovative initiative launched by the World Bank, in collaboration with the United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees (UNHCR). This project aimed to empower refugees and promote sustainable development in our community

Challenges

As young and ambitious farmers, we were eager to take on the challenge. However, we soon encountered various obstacles, including difficulties in controlling temperature and humidity levels, predator attacks on our larvae, and managing feed quality for our larvae .

Over view

Despite these challenges, we persevered through our collective hard work, determination, and commitment to seeking knowledge. We consulted with experts, attended workshops, and followed guidelines from reputable agricultural institutions.

Impact Change

Our ultimate goal is to establish a sustainable and reliable source of protein and nutrients for our livestock. By utilizing black soldier fly larvae, we aim to reduce waste management issues in our area and improve soil fertility through the application of natural manure.
